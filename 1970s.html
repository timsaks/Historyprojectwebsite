The 1970s

The 1970s brought political distrust, economic strain, environmental awareness, and new questions about America's role at home and in the world.

1974

Watergate and Resignation

The Watergate scandal ended with President Richard Nixon resigning from office. It damaged public trust in government and showed the importance of investigations, journalism, and checks on executive power.

1979

Energy Crisis

Gas shortages and rising energy prices forced Americans to think differently about consumption, foreign oil, and the economy. The crisis revealed how global events could directly affect everyday life.
