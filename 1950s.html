Decade Exhibit

The 1950s

The decade is often remembered for suburban growth and consumer culture, but it also contained major challenges to segregation and traditional expectations.

1954

Brown v. Board of Education

The Supreme Court ruled that segregated public schools were unconstitutional. Sites like Monroe Elementary connect the decision to real students and families, and the ruling became a major legal foundation for the Civil Rights Movement.

1957

Rock and Roll Culture

Rock and roll brought rhythm and blues, country, and youth culture into the mainstream. Artists like Elvis Presley showed how music could challenge older ideas about race, behavior, and popular entertainment.
