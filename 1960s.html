The 1960s

Americans pushed for civil rights, questioned authority, fought over Vietnam, and watched technology make the Moon a reachable destination.

1963

The Feminine Mystique

More than 200,000 people gathered in Washington, D.C. to demand civil and economic rights. The march showed the strength of organized protest and helped build pressure for major civil rights legislation.

1964

Civil Rights Act (Title VII)

The Apollo 11 mission fulfilled President Kennedy's goal of landing Americans on the Moon. It became a symbol of scientific ambition, Cold War competition, and national confidence during a divided decade.
